Gobblet


Rules

Goal

Align four pieces in a row, horizontally, vertically, or diagonally

Set-Up

Each player has 3 stacks of four pieces of varying sizes. Only the largest piece is visible. However when this piece is removed it uncovers the next piece of a smaller size underneath it and so on and so forth.

Play

Players take turns moving. The blue player goes first. For each turn a player can either

  • Put a new piece on the board from their stack.
  • Move one of their pieces on the board to an empty space
  • Gobble up a smaller size piece on the board with a larger piece of their color that is also on the board

There is an exception to the second rule. If the opponent has 3 pieces in a row, (including diagonals that aren't part of a four-in-a-row line) you may gobble up one of the pieces in the line with a piece from your own stack

Notes

  • Players are allowed to gobble their own pieces, but they can't do so using a piece from their own stack
  • If no one has any pieces left in their stack play continues using the pieces on the board.
  • You can't move a piece back onto your own stack
  • Once a piece is selected it must be played
